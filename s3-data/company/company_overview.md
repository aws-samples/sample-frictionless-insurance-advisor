# Unicorn Insurance Company Overview

## About Us

Unicorn Insurance is a multi-line insurance carrier offering life, health, motor, home, travel, disability, and critical illness cover to individuals and families. We distribute through independent advisors, tied agents, employee benefits brokers, and a direct digital channel. Our model pairs underwriting discipline with modern technology, and we operate as a single international group under a common brand.

This document covers who we are, where we operate, and what we stand for. Product details sit in our portfolio documentation.

## History

### Founding

Unicorn Insurance was founded in 2008 in Singapore by a group of former reinsurance and technology executives. The founders set out to build a digitally native carrier at a time when most incumbents still ran on mainframe policy systems. The first product line, motor insurance, launched in Singapore in late 2008.

### Key milestones

- **2008** — Company incorporated in Singapore. First motor policies written.
- **2010** — Expansion into the United Kingdom and launch of our term life portfolio.
- **2012** — Entry into continental Europe (Germany, France, Netherlands, Spain) and launch of home insurance.
- **2014** — Health and critical illness lines introduced across European markets.
- **2016** — APAC expansion begins with offices in Singapore and Hong Kong.
- **2018** — Launch of our first unified mobile app and self-service customer portal.
- **2020** — North American expansion with licensed carriers in Canada and select U.S. states.
- **2022** — Travel and disability portfolios rolled out group-wide.
- **2024** — Reached 7.2 million active policyholders across all markets.

We publish an annual report each March with audited figures. The numbers above are round-figure summaries for advisor reference.

## Headquarters and Offices

### Group headquarters

Our registered head office is in Singapore. It houses group functions including executive leadership, group actuarial, group risk, finance, and technology.

### Regional hubs

We operate four regional hubs that serve the markets around them:

- **APAC hub** — Singapore (also the group HQ).
- **EMEA hub** — Dublin, Ireland.
- **UK & Nordics hub** — London, United Kingdom.
- **Americas hub** — Toronto, Canada.

Each hub runs local underwriting, claims, customer service, and distribution for its region. Regional leaders report into the group executive team.

### Country offices

In addition to the hubs, we maintain country offices in markets where we write material business, including Germany, France, Spain, Italy, the Netherlands, Poland, the United Arab Emirates, Hong Kong, Australia, and selected U.S. states operated through our licensed U.S. subsidiary.

## Geographic Footprint

We are licensed to write insurance business in 22 countries across three regions:

- **Europe (14 markets)** — Ireland, United Kingdom, Germany, France, Spain, Italy, the Netherlands, Belgium, Luxembourg, Portugal, Poland, Sweden, Denmark, Norway.
- **Asia-Pacific (5 markets)** — Singapore, Hong Kong SAR, Australia, New Zealand, United Arab Emirates.
- **Americas (3 markets)** — Canada (all provinces), United States (licensed in 14 states as of the current filing), and Mexico (select lines through a local partner).

Total active policies: approximately 7.2 million. Gross written premium in the most recent reporting year: approximately $4.8 billion.

### Reinsurance partners

We cede portions of our portfolio to a panel of global reinsurers rated A or better. This supports our capital position and lets us write larger individual risks without concentrating exposure.

## Our Mission

> To make insurance clearer, faster, and fairer, so our customers can get on with the things that matter to them.

We read the mission as a short list of operating commitments:

- **Clearer** — policy wordings that a non-specialist can understand, with plain-language summaries alongside the legal text.
- **Faster** — quote, bind, and claim journeys measured in minutes rather than days where the risk permits.
- **Fairer** — pricing that reflects the individual risk, not opaque cross-subsidies, and claims decisions grounded in the policy terms.

## Our Values

We use five values to guide day-to-day decisions. Advisors may see these referenced in our marketing materials and training.

### 1. Customer first

Every product, process, and policy wording is reviewed against a simple test: does it leave the customer better informed and better protected than before? When trade-offs arise, we resolve them in favor of the customer where the risk and regulatory framework allow.

### 2. Straight talk

We write policies, claims letters, and advisor briefings in plain language. We disclose exclusions upfront rather than in footnotes. When we make a mistake, we say so and fix it.

### 3. Evidence over opinion

Underwriting, pricing, and claims decisions are grounded in data, actuarial analysis, and the policy wording. We document the reasoning behind material decisions so they can be reviewed.

### 4. Responsible risk

We only write risks we understand and price to the expected cost of claims, expenses, and a reasonable return on capital. We decline business that does not meet our underwriting standards rather than chase volume.

### 5. One team

Group, regional, and country teams operate under a shared playbook. Advisors working with us in one market should recognize the same service standards in another.

## Governance and Ownership

Unicorn Insurance is a private group. The group holding company, Unicorn Insurance Holdings Limited, is majority-owned by a consortium of long-term institutional investors, with a minority stake held by current and former employees through an employee share plan. We are not listed on a public exchange.

The group is overseen by a board of directors that includes the Group CEO, the Group CFO, the Chief Risk Officer, and seven non-executive directors. Board committees cover audit, risk, remuneration, and customer outcomes.

## Sustainability and Community

We run a group-wide sustainability program covering our own operations and our underwriting and investment portfolios. Current commitments include:

- Net-zero operational emissions by 2030.
- Alignment of the group investment portfolio with a net-zero pathway by 2040.
- Annual disclosure against the TCFD framework, published with our financial statements.

We also fund a community grants program that supports financial-literacy and road-safety initiatives in the markets where we operate.
