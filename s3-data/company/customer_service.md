# Unicorn Insurance Customer Service

This document describes how customers reach us, when we are available, what languages we support, and how complaints are escalated. Advisors can use it to set accurate expectations during the sales conversation and to hand off existing customers to the right channel.

## Support Channels

We run a multi-channel service model. Customers choose the channel that fits the question; advisors can do the same on behalf of their clients where the customer has given consent.

### Phone

- **General customer service:** a local number in each market, published on the country website and on the back of the policy schedule. Calls route to the regional hub that owns the customer's policy.
- **Claims hotline:** a dedicated 24/7 number, separate from general service, for reporting new claims and emergency assistance. See the Claims section below for the numbers.
- **Advisor desk:** a priority line for licensed advisors to handle policy questions, mid-term adjustments, and claims escalations on behalf of their clients. Advisors receive the number at onboarding.

Wait-time target for general service is under 90 seconds during business hours. The claims hotline answers on average within 30 seconds.

### Email

- **General enquiries:** `support@unicorninsurance.example`
- **Claims:** `claims@unicorninsurance.example`
- **Advisors:** `advisors@unicorninsurance.example`
- **Complaints:** `complaints@unicorninsurance.example`
- **Data protection / privacy:** `privacy@unicorninsurance.example`

We acknowledge email within one business day and aim to resolve or provide a substantive update within three business days.

### Live chat

- Available through the customer portal and mobile app.
- Staffed by human agents during business hours, with an AI-assisted self-service assistant available 24/7 for common questions (policy documents, premium due dates, address changes, basic coverage queries).
- Chat transcripts are saved to the customer's account for reference.

### In-app messaging

- Secure messaging inside the Unicorn Insurance mobile app. Messages are tied to the customer's authenticated session and are persisted, so a conversation can span multiple sessions.
- Push notifications let customers know when we reply.
- Photo and document upload is supported inside the chat, useful for claims evidence, proof of no-claims, and address documents.

### Branch and advisor visits

- Face-to-face service is available through our tied agents and partner advisors, and at our regional hub offices by appointment.
- We do not operate a large retail branch network; most servicing is digital or by phone.

### Post

- Written correspondence is accepted at each country office. Addresses are listed on the country website and on policy documents.
- Legal and regulatory notices should go to the registered address of the relevant group entity.

## Service Hours

Hours are expressed in the customer's local time.

### General customer service

- **Monday to Friday:** 08:00 to 20:00
- **Saturday:** 09:00 to 17:00
- **Sunday and public holidays:** closed for general service; urgent matters route to the claims hotline.

### Claims hotline

- **24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year.** This includes public holidays.
- Emergency assistance (roadside, medical, travel) is staffed by our assistance partners and also operates 24/7.

### Advisor desk

- **Monday to Friday:** 08:00 to 19:00 local time.
- Out-of-hours urgent advisor issues can be routed through the claims hotline.

### Digital self-service

- The customer portal and mobile app are available 24/7, subject to planned maintenance windows which are published in advance.

## Claims Hotline

The claims hotline is the fastest way to report a new claim or get emergency assistance.

- **Europe claims hotline:** local numbers published on the policy schedule; a pan-European freephone is available for customers traveling in the EEA.
- **UK claims hotline:** dedicated UK freephone.
- **Singapore and Hong Kong claims hotline:** local numbers.
- **Australia claims hotline:** 1300-series local number.
- **Canada and U.S. claims hotline:** toll-free North American number.
- **International travel emergency assistance:** a worldwide reverse-charge number printed on every travel policy certificate.

When a customer calls the claims hotline, we aim to:

1. Open the claim and issue a claim reference in the same call.
2. Arrange emergency services (towing, medical assistance, temporary accommodation) where the policy provides for it.
3. Tell the customer exactly what documents we need next and how to upload them.

The claims process itself is covered in the claims documentation.

## Escalation Path

If a customer is not satisfied with the response they receive, we follow a structured escalation path. Advisors should know the steps so they can coach clients.

### Step 1 — Front-line agent

The customer raises the issue through any support channel. The agent is empowered to resolve most issues directly. If they cannot resolve it, they log it as a formal complaint.

### Step 2 — Team leader review

The agent's team leader reviews the complaint, contacts the customer within two business days, and issues a decision within ten business days. Most complaints close at this stage.

### Step 3 — Customer Relations Office

Unresolved complaints move to our central Customer Relations Office. A dedicated case handler, independent of the original team, reviews the file. We issue a final response letter within the regulatory deadline that applies in the customer's market (for example, eight weeks in the UK under FCA rules, 40 business days in Ireland under Central Bank rules).

### Step 4 — Independent ombudsman

If the customer remains dissatisfied, the final response letter explains how to refer the complaint to the independent dispute-resolution scheme in their market. The relevant schemes include:

- Financial Services and Pensions Ombudsman (FSPO) — Ireland
- Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) — United Kingdom
- Financial Industry Disputes Resolution Centre (FIDReC) — Singapore
- Australian Financial Complaints Authority (AFCA) — Australia
- General Insurance OmbudService and OmbudService for Life and Health Insurance — Canada
- State Department of Insurance complaints offices — United States

Referral to the ombudsman is free for the customer, and our decision remains with us until the ombudsman reviews the case.

### Advisor-initiated escalation

Advisors can escalate on behalf of a client (with the client's consent) by contacting the advisor desk or emailing `complaints@unicorninsurance.example` with the policy or claim reference. The same SLAs apply.
