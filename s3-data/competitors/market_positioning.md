# Competitor Market Positioning Overview

> **NOTICE:** The content in this document is illustrative and represents a fictional competitor used for demo and training purposes. It does not represent any real company. Advisors must not quote this content as factual information about real insurers in any customer-facing conversation.

## Purpose of this document

This document helps Unicorn Insurance advisors understand how three fictional competitor archetypes — **BigRival**, **StarInsure**, and **QuickSafe** — are positioned in the broader insurance market, and where Unicorn Insurance sits relative to each of them. Use it as a mental map before comparing individual products. For line-by-line product comparisons, refer to the per-competitor files: `bigrival.md`, `starinsure.md`, and `quicksafe.md`.

The archetypes are not real companies. They are composite profiles chosen to match common customer reference points, so advisors can respond consistently when prospects say things like "I already have coverage with a big legacy carrier," "I just want the cheapest app-based policy," or "I've been using one of those modern insurtech brands."

## The three archetypes at a glance

### BigRival — the incumbent legacy carrier

BigRival represents the long-established, broad-portfolio national carrier. Customers recognise the brand, agents are abundant, and the product range spans nearly every personal and small-commercial line. The trade-off is premium pricing, slower claims cycles, and a digital experience that lags behind newer entrants. BigRival is frequently the provider a prospect is leaving when they first speak to a Unicorn advisor.

### StarInsure — the direct-to-consumer, price-led challenger

StarInsure represents the budget-focused, fully self-service online insurer. Policies are purchased through an app or website in minutes, coverage is deliberately narrow, and there is no human advisor in the buying journey. StarInsure wins on headline price but typically excludes higher-value add-ons, offers lower coverage caps, and provides limited support when claims become complex.

### QuickSafe — the modern insurtech

QuickSafe represents the well-funded insurtech brand with strong app design, fast quoting, and a narrow but well-executed product range. The customer experience is polished and mobile-first, but the product portfolio is shallow — typically only three or four lines — and coverage depth within each line is more limited than at established carriers. QuickSafe customers often value convenience and design over breadth.

## Where Unicorn Insurance sits

Unicorn Insurance is positioned as a **breadth-plus-experience** provider: the product range covers the same core lines as a legacy carrier (life, health, motor, home, disability, travel, critical illness), the digital experience is comparable to a modern insurtech, and advisors remain available throughout the customer lifecycle rather than only at onboarding.

In short:

- Against BigRival, Unicorn competes on digital experience, claims speed, and mid-market pricing while matching product breadth.
- Against StarInsure, Unicorn competes on coverage depth, optional riders, and access to human advisors while remaining price-competitive in the mid-market tier.
- Against QuickSafe, Unicorn competes on product breadth and bundling while matching the app experience.

## How to use this positioning in conversations

Advisors should anchor comparisons to **what the customer actually values**, not to the competitor's weakest point. The table above is a framing tool, not a talking-points script.

- If the prospect mentions a **legacy carrier** and cites slow claims or outdated tools, position Unicorn on digital experience and claims responsiveness while acknowledging that both carriers cover the same product lines.
- If the prospect mentions a **budget, app-only provider** and cites low premium, acknowledge the price point and pivot to coverage depth, advisor access, and bundling discounts that offset the headline price difference.
- If the prospect mentions a **modern insurtech** and cites app experience, match on digital experience and pivot to product breadth — especially if the prospect has needs outside the insurtech's handful of lines.

## Positioning summary

Unicorn Insurance is designed to be the provider that a customer does not have to compromise with. Customers coming from BigRival get a comparable product range with faster service and better digital tools. Customers coming from StarInsure get real coverage depth and advisor support without paying a premium tier. Customers coming from QuickSafe get the same app-quality experience plus the four or five additional product lines that QuickSafe does not offer.

Advisors should lead with this framing — breadth plus experience, priced for the mid-market — rather than attacking any individual competitor. Every competitor in this document serves a real customer segment well; the goal is to show why Unicorn serves more segments, more fully, more of the time.
