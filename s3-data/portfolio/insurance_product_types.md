# Insurance Product Types

## Life Insurance
Life insurance provides financial protection to beneficiaries upon the death of the insured. It can help cover funeral expenses, replace lost income, pay off debts, and provide for dependents' future needs.

Life insurance comes in four primary forms, each with different cost, cash-value behavior, and flexibility characteristics:

- **Term Life Insurance** provides coverage for a fixed period (typically 10 to 30 years). Premiums are low relative to permanent products, but the policy has no cash value and expires at the end of the term unless renewed or converted. Suited to customers who need large coverage amounts during a specific window such as raising children or paying off a mortgage.
- **Whole Life Insurance** provides permanent coverage for the insured's entire life. Premiums are level and typically higher than term, the death benefit is guaranteed, and the policy builds cash value at a contractually guaranteed rate. Suited to estate planning, lifetime legacy needs, and customers who value predictable costs and guaranteed cash accumulation.
- **Universal Life Insurance** provides permanent coverage with flexible premiums and an adjustable death benefit. Cash value earns interest at a declared rate with a guaranteed minimum floor. Suited to customers who want lifelong coverage but expect their cash flow or protection needs to change over time.
- **Variable Life Insurance** provides permanent coverage combined with cash value invested in policyholder-selected sub-accounts, similar to mutual funds. Values fluctuate with market performance, so the product carries investment risk alongside the insurance benefit. Suited to financially sophisticated customers with long time horizons who want market-linked growth inside a tax-advantaged life insurance structure.

## Critical Illness Insurance
Critical illness insurance provides a lump sum payment upon diagnosis of specific serious conditions like cancer, heart attack, or stroke. This payment can be used for treatment costs, lifestyle adjustments, or income replacement.

## Health Insurance
Health insurance covers medical expenses incurred due to illness or injury. It typically includes doctor visits, hospital stays, surgeries, and prescription medications, helping to manage healthcare costs.

## Home Insurance
Home insurance protects your property and possessions against damage or theft. It typically covers the building structure, personal belongings, and liability for accidents that occur on the property.

## Motor Insurance
Motor insurance provides financial protection against physical damage and bodily injury resulting from traffic accidents. It also covers liability that could arise from vehicle incidents.

## Travel Insurance
Travel insurance covers unexpected events during domestic or international travel, including medical emergencies, trip cancellations, lost luggage, and travel delays.
